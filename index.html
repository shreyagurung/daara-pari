Welcome to Daarapari

My ancestral home in Hee Bermiok, West Sikkim.

For generations, my family has lived here, growing our own food, working on the farm, and living alongside our neighbours. Every day here is different, shaped by the seasons.

This isn't a hotel. It's a home that's always had room for one more person at the table.

Whether you're here to take a break, work remotely, write, paint, read, or simply spend time doing nothing, I hope you'll slow down.

Watch the clouds.
Walk through the fields.
Eat what's growing in the garden.
Spend an evening around the fire.
Get to know the village.
Stay long enough for this place to feel familiar.

Welcome home.
- Sristi :)
The Spaces

Honest Simplicity

I built our mud house with the actual earth they stand on. It is raw, unpolished, and warmed by the fire, so don't expect marble floors, but do expect very cozy toes.

Hands in the soil

I invite you to connect with where your food comes from (yes, that means getting your hands dirty). Join me in the fields, walk through the mist, and let the quietness settle in.

What we grow here, and what we gather from nearby, shapes everything we eat and share. Some of it even travels a little further.

A Day Here

No alarms. No plans. Just the way I live every day.

Morning

Mornings take their own time here. They might begin long after noon. No plans, no straight lines, just the hills slowly setting the rhythm of your day.

Afternoon

You can walk the village paths or just sit on the balcony. Time softens here. You might finally read that book you've been pretending to read for years.

Evening

We all eat together. I'll serve home-cooked meals straight from our farm. The fire crackles, and somehow, you won't even miss your phone notifications.

The Right Fit

Who finds their way here

  • Couples & solo travelers seeking deep rest over constant activity.
  • Those comfortable with nature's rhythm - mist, rain, bugs, and quiet.
  • Creators and urban professionals experiencing burnout.
  • People looking for uncurated, authentic village immersion.
  • Anyone who wants to eat farm-grown food and do absolutely nothing.
Setting Expectations

Who might prefer elsewhere

  • Traditional luxury seekers expecting room service and resort amenities.
  • Travelers with rigid itineraries looking to "maximize" sightseeing.
  • Loud groups or those looking for a party atmosphere.
  • Those uncomfortable with the honest realities of rural farm life.
