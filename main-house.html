Mul Ghar

A house we live in, with extra rooms for the people who come to stay.

Private & shared rooms · Dorm · Shared living spaces

₹1200 – ₹1800 per person · Meals included

The living room is the centre of things.

There's a low table that has held many cups of tea, several arguments, two weddings worth of planning, and at least one nap a day. Guests tend to gather here in the evenings, mostly because that's where the warmth is.

A dorm, for those travelling light.

Four beds, clean sheets, a window that faces the hill. It's the simplest way to be here, and honestly some of our favourite conversations have started in this room at an unreasonable hour.

Meals happen at one table.

Brunch and dinner are cooked in the same kitchen and eaten together. That’s just how people here have always eaten. One proper meal in the morning before the day begins, and another when the work is done. What’s on the table comes from the garden when it can, and from nearby when it doesn’t. We don’t ask anyone to stay quiet, but the conversation is usually softer than you’d expect.

It is, first, a farm.

If you want to help out, we'll happily put a tool in your hand. If you'd rather watch the work happen from the verandah with a second cup of coffee, we understand that too. Both are honest ways to spend a morning.

Evenings are mostly outside.

When the light goes, people drift to the verandah. Someone usually brings out a blanket. Someone usually brings out music, eventually. Then it gets cold, and we go in.

There are a few different ways to stay in the main house. Pick whatever sounds right, or write to us and we'll suggest.
